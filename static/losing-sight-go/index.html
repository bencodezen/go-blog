Losing Sight of My Go

By BenGoZen
In Journal, Kifu
June 24, 2014
2 Min read
15 comments
L

Today I played a game against a 2k as Black (with 45 min main time settings). It’s been a long time since I’ve played a game with such long time settings where the opponent actually used up most of his/her time. In fact, it was a little nerve wracking because the quality of the moves were quite different. Far less impulsive ones and much more proper moves that were given a lot of thought. I felt I had decent control of the game for the most part, but started losing control of it towards the later half of the middle game. Around this time, my opponent misclicks.
At first I was thrilled to see the mistake, but then the undo request instantly popped up and I was confronted with the conflicting decision as to wheter I should give him the undo or not.Â Long story short,Â he was supposed to gain about 8 points in sente, but misclicked and put his own stones in atari so that I could capture them instead, save my stones, and gain a few points myself.

For 95% of spectators out there, the answer is crystal clear: it was an obvious misclick. In that moment however,Â though I’m ashamed to say it, I wanted to win so badly that I tried to brush off his undo and play on. I tried to resolve my cognitive dissonance by telling myselfÂ that it was his fault that he misclicked. Not my problem. Within a few moves however, I woke to my senses and realized how pitiful I was being. So I resigned in order to try and pick up what littleÂ self-respect I had left.
The reason I’m writing about this is because I realized I have become too caught up in winning. Even though my name was created with the idea that I had found zen in go, I think that it has become so far from the truth (especially as of late). With each game recently, any sense of calmness or zen has been consistentlyÂ disturbed by the notion of winning or losing. And with each game, I am concerned that I am losing sight of my go.
This ends now. I don’t care if I have to drop ten stones to get back to it, but I’m going to putÂ the pieces that started this blog back together:Â Ben + Go + Zen.

      Well in this case it was a very obvious misclick. And though I understand the perspective of “no undo, even if obvious misclick,” I personally would like to win my games because I am stronger and not because of a misclick. Of course it would be ridiculous to give multiple undos to an opponent who keeps asking for it, but I think not giving obvious misclicks is kind of bad sportsmanship.
      In fact, the perfect scene to depict this is Hikaru vs Isumi in the pro exam. He wanted the win so bad he took the forfeit, but it ended up hurting him more in the end because he couldn’t gain back his mentality. So personally, I feel that players should be confident in their own abilities and believe in themselves instead of trying to win through a misclick.
      And it’s not so much that wanting to win is bad, but being attached to the idea of winning and being angry at losing is what I am drawing issue with. It’s emotional baggage that is unnecessary for getting stronger.
      I won’t be taking a break for now. The meditation and breathing techniques are fine before the game, but it’s during the game where I have problems. So that’s what I have to continue working on. After all, you can’t really meditate in the middle of a game. I’d run out of time otherwise. Haha.
