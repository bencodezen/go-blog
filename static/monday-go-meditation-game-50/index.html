    • Ben says:
      April 8, 2014 at 7:44 am

      Thanks! I’m glad that the questions are understandable and that you are getting them right.
      In response to your question about the lower right, if White R1, Black would definitely T2. However, if White plays S2, White is actually just alive. The reason for this is because Black can never approach White to kill him (White has one extra liberty). However, White can always kill the Black stones whenever he chooses (if some referee demands it to be played out or something).
      On the other hand, if White does NOT play S2, then Black can play S2 and form an eye. At that point, then it will be seki because neither Black nor White will be able to approach one another without dying.
      Does that clarify the position? And to your last point, the fact that you could enunciate your thoughts so clearly means you certainly do understand the rules of this crazy game. It’s just a matter of life and death clarifying. That’s all. =D
